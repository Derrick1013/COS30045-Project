Understanding the Graph

The Medical Graduate Rates Choropleth Map visualizes the distribution of medical graduates per 100,000 people across various OECD countries, showing how these rates have changed over time. This map provides a geographically grounded view of medical graduate rates, helping us to identify regional patterns, disparities, and trends that might influence the healthcare workforce globally.

Why This Graph Was Chosen

A choropleth map is particularly effective for this type of analysis because it leverages both spatial and quantitative data, allowing users to see medical graduate rates in the context of each country's location and size. By color-coding countries based on their medical graduate rate, we can immediately discern areas with high or low output of new medical professionals. This visualization helps us explore how each nation contributes to the overall healthcare workforce and understand which regions may be better positioned to meet healthcare demands based on their graduate output.

Data source

The map combines data from two main sources: GeoJSON files for geographic country boundaries, and OECD Health Statistics for the medical graduates data. The OECD dataset includes values for medical graduates per 100,000 people, organized by country and year. By mapping each country’s rate over time, we gain valuable insights into the healthcare workforce trends and potential regional challenges in medical education. This choropleth map provides a geographic lens on the global healthcare workforce, showing which regions are producing medical graduates at rates that may sustain or improve healthcare access over time.
